Questions

Common questions

When will ChainSync be available?

We're currently in active development and don't have a public launch date yet. We're prioritizing building the system correctly over launching quickly. Early access testing will begin when our core agent coordination is reliable and thoroughly tested. Join our early access list to be notified when testing opportunities become available.

Who is ChainSync designed for?

We're building ChainSync for environmental agencies, water treatment facilities, industrial sites with environmental monitoring requirements, and municipalities managing environmental health. Anyone responsible for detecting and responding to environmental emergencies could benefit from automated coordination.

What makes the Python agent approach different?

Instead of a single monolithic system trying to do everything, we're building specialized Python agents that each excel at one task (detection, analysis, coordination, documentation). This modular approach makes the system more reliable, easier to update, and more adaptable to different scenarios. If one agent needs improvement, we can update it without disrupting the entire system.

How can I get involved or stay updated?

Join our early access list through the contact form. We'll share development updates, testing opportunities, and ways to provide input on features as we build. We're particularly interested in hearing from environmental professionals who deal with emergency response coordination.

Will this replace human responders?

No. ChainSync is designed to augment human expertise, not replace it. The system handles coordination, notifications, and documentation‚Äîthe administrative overhead that slows down response. This frees up environmental professionals to focus on what they do best: making decisions and taking action to protect communities and ecosystems.
